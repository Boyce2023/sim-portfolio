## Company-by-Company Detail

### 1. META — Q1 2026
**Capex:** $19.84B (incl. finance lease principal payments)
**Q1 2025 baseline:** $13.69B
**YoY:** +45%
**FY26 Guidance (updated Q1):** $125B–$145B (raised from $115B–$135B prior)
**Context:** FY25 full-year was $72.2B — FY26 guide implies near-doubling in a single year

**Management Language (Zuckerberg, 2026-04-29):**
> "We're building toward what I think of as AGI and then AI superintelligence. The infrastructure we're building now will define the next decade."
> CFO cited "higher memory component pricing and additional data center costs for future capacity" as drivers of the $10B midpoint guidance raise.

**AI%:** ~100% — all new capex directed at AI training infrastructure and data center capacity

**Market Reaction:** Stock -6% after-hours on capex raise despite revenue +33% YoY, net income +61%

### 2. MICROSOFT — Q3 FY2026 (calendar Q1 2026, ended March 31)
**Capex:** $31.9B (incl. finance leases) — down sequentially from Q2 FY26 due to timing
**Q3 FY2025 baseline (same quarter prior year):** $21.4B
**YoY:** +49%
**FY26 Full Calendar Year Guidance:** ~$190B (Amy Hood), of which ~$25B attributed to component price inflation
**Q4 FY26 (calendar Q2) guidance:** >$40B

**Management Language (Amy Hood / Satya Nadella, 2026-04-29):**
> "Roughly two-thirds of this quarter's CapEx was for short-lived assets — primarily GPUs and CPUs — the remaining spend supports long-lived assets monetizable over 15+ years."
> Hood: "We expect Q4 CapEx to rise above $40B as demand signals remain persistent."

**Azure AI:** Run rate >$37B annualized, Azure grew 40% YoY. AI business run rate >$3B.
**Margin note:** Gross margin fell to 67.6% (from 68.7%) — lowest since 2022, driven by GPU depreciation acceleration

**Market Reaction:** Stock fell despite Azure +40% beat — $190B CY26 capex guide well above $152B analyst estimate

### 3. ALPHABET (GOOGL) — Q1 2026
**Capex:** $35.7B (servers, DC, real estate, other infra)
**Q1 2025 baseline:** ~$17.2B (Q1 2025 actual, per quarterly trend data)
**YoY:** ~+107% (largest YoY jump of the four)
**FY26 Guidance (updated Q1):** $180B–$190B (raised from $175B–$185B prior guidance)
**2027 signal:** CFO Ashkenazi explicitly guided 2027 capex to "significantly increase" vs 2026

**Management Language (Sundar Pichai / Anat Ashkenazi, 2026-04-29):**
> Pichai: "We are compute constrained in the near term — cloud revenue would have been higher if we could meet demand."
> Ashkenazi: "The overwhelming majority of our CapEx spend is in technical infrastructure to support the AI opportunities we see."
> Pichai: "2026 is off to a terrific start. Our AI investments and full stack approach are lighting up every part of the business."

**Google Cloud:** Revenue +63% YoY to ~$13.5B. Backlog nearly doubled to $462B.
**Demand signal:** "Cloud revenue would have been higher" = supply-constrained, not demand-constrained. Strongest demand signal of the four.

**Market Reaction:** Stock positive — investors rewarded the "AI is actually paying off" narrative (Cloud +63%, backlog doubled)

### 4. AMAZON — Q1 2026
**Capex:** $44.2B (DC, networking, custom chips, physical infrastructure)
**Q1 2025 baseline:** $25.0B
**YoY:** +77%
**FY26 Full Year Committed:** ~$200B (largest single-year infrastructure commitment in corporate history)

**Management Language (Andy Jassy, 2026-04-29):**
> "We invest ahead of demand because cash outlays for land, power, buildings, and hardware happen 6 to 24 months before we bill customers. This is how we built the first wave of AWS — heavy upfront spending built the capacity that funded a decade of compounding returns."
> On Trainium: "At scale, we expect Trainium to save tens of billions of dollars in capex annually and deliver several hundred basis points of operating margin advantage for inference versus relying on external chips."

**AWS:** Revenue +28% YoY to $37.6B — fastest pace since late 2022. Backlog $364B, of which >$225B is Trainium revenue commitments.
**FCF:** Collapsed ~95% QoQ due to capex ramp (Anthropic $16.8B gain inflated net income optics)

**Market Reaction:** Stock -3% after-hours despite beating every earnings line — investors digesting $200B commitment

## Cycle Judgment: ACCELERATING

**Verdict: The capex cycle is accelerating, not plateauing.**

Three signals confirm this:

**1. All four raised or confirmed their guides in Q1 2026**
No company guided down. GOOGL and META both raised guides mid-year. MSFT guided Q4 above $40B sequentially.

**2. Demand is supply-constrained at the infra layer**
GOOGL's "cloud revenue would have been higher" is the clearest signal. AWS backlog $364B (+$225B Trainium commitments). MSFT Azure backlog growing. These are not speculative — they are contracted demand that cannot be met yet.

**3. 2027 is already signaled as another step-up**
GOOGL CFO explicitly called 2027 capex "significantly higher" than 2026. AMZN's investment horizon language (6-24 month lag) implies current spending funds 2027-2028 revenue. This is not a one-year burst.

**What would change the read:**
- Sequential capex decline in Q2 2026 without revenue acceleration
- Hyperscaler guidance cuts tied to AI ROI disappointment (not yet visible — Cloud growth is accelerating: AWS +28%, GCP +63%)
- Macro shock forcing capital rationing (not signaled)

|-------|--------|------------|
